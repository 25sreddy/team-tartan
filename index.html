The Opportunity

CMU is a high-pressure academic environment where loneliness and social isolation are documented concerns. Students want to host events, join pickup sports, and meet people — but there's no single place to do it. Discovery is scattered across Discord servers, flyers, and word-of-mouth, systematically disadvantaging new, international, and transfer students.

No central platform

No central platform for informal events.

Pickup games fall apart

Pickup games fall apart without enough players.

New students stay on the outside

New students can't break into social circles.

Fragmented discovery

Event discovery is fragmented and unreliable.

Our Solution

A real-time, student-centered web platform where any verified CMU student (via Andrew ID SSO) can post a spontaneous or planned activity — pickup basketball, poker night, movie watch party, study session — and watch it appear instantly in a live campus feed.

Theoretical Foundations

Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

Adoption is driven by perceived usefulness and perceived ease of use.

Applied to Team Tartan: SSO login supports ease of use; real events with real peers drive usefulness. Risk: low early event volume hurts perceived usefulness. Mitigation: seeded launch with student orgs.

Sociotechnical Systems Theory

Technology and social systems must co-evolve.

Applied to Team Tartan: We do not only digitize event posting — we reshape how students discover community. CMU's culture of academic intensity and club fragmentation is the social system the platform must work within and gradually shift.

Equity & Inclusion Framework

Not all students enter CMU with equal social capital. International, graduate, and underrepresented students face higher integration barriers.

Applied to Team Tartan: Anonymous RSVP mode; no follower counts or popularity signals; no algorithmic amplification of already-popular orgs; low-stakes drop-in event formats.

  • Student orgs gain a lightweight, frictionless outreach channel.
  • Intramural teams can reliably fill pickup game rosters.
  • CMU can demonstrate proactive investment in student wellbeing.

Societal Impact

  • Reduces documented social isolation at a high-pressure institution.
  • Lowers barriers for marginalized student populations (international, transfer, underrepresented).
  • Builds a replicable model for campus social infrastructure at other universities.
  • Creates lasting peer connections that extend beyond individual events.
